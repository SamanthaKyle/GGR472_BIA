# Take the Scenic Route
*by Juliette Adams Fong, Samantha Kyle, and Emmett Young*

*Created for The Beach BIA under the supervision of Dr. Johanna Carlo*

## Introduction
Take the Scenic Route is a web-based mapping tool that guides users on how best to spend their time visiting Toronto's Beach neighbourhood.
We created seven routes showcasing different assets of the Beaches - from diverse green spaces, to lively third spaces, and the vibrant local businesses. The routes take a user on one of three kinds of adventures: fully natural, fully commercial, and mixed natural-commercial adventures. Each route involves several stops where users can explore and deeply get to know this neighbourhood - by spotting local birds, supporting small businesses, admiring historical landmarks, or experiencing the food scene.

The goal of this website is to change how Beachgoers experience this space and encourage them to break existing habits and patterns through exploration, discovery, wandering, and getting lost. This tool was commissioned by the Beach BIA as a part of their larger Smart Parks initiative, which aims to activate public spaces using data-informed planning tools.

This website draws heavily on public data from the City of Toronto Open Data Portal, iNaturalist observation data, Open Street Maps amenity data, and more. The mapping interface is facilitated through MapBox-gl-js, and the general interface is created using the Bootstrap Framework.

Read more below to understand how to best engage with Take the Scenic Route.

## How do you *Take the Scenic Route?*

Follow the GitHub Pages link to see the tool: https://samanthakyle.github.io/GGR472_BIA/
*It will be migrating to the Beach BIA official website before the end of April*

Map controls are on the right, allowing you to zoom in, zoom out, reset orientation to North, full-screen, or use the geocoder to find an address on the map.

By default, the map shows the outline of the Beach neighbourhood as well as a variety of amenities. Green P Parking, BikeShare docks, and public washrooms are visible from any extent. Clicking on a public washroom icon will yield more information about the washroom's hours of operation and accessibility accommodations. When zooming in closer, icons representing bus stops and water fountains become visible as well.

Scroll through the cards on the left to see the premade routes.
Hovering over a card brings a sidebar with more information about the route and prompts the map to zoom to the route's extent, showing the route as a line and nodes on the map.

Clicking a card will 'select' the route - allowing the information card and route to stay on the screen and be interacted with more closely.
Explore more information about each stop along the way by hovering or clicking each node.

When you are ready to choose a route for your adventure, click the 'Take This Route' button at the bottom of the card to be taken to Google Maps. From there, if you are logged in to a Google Account, you will be shown the option to send the route directly to your phone so you can take it on the move.

The bottom left card on the website is a nod to the next phase of development. Find Your Adventure is the next iteration of the tool, which will allow users to select from collections of places in the Beaches (restaurants, pubs, green spaces, art galleries, etc.) to drag-and-drop a custom route. This tool is still 'Coming Soon'!
